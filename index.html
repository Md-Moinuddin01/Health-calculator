Understanding BMI

What BMI means

Body mass index compares weight with height. It is calculated as kilograms divided by metres squared, or 703 × pounds divided by inches squared.

Adult categories

Below 18.5: underweight; 18.5–24.9: healthy; 25.0–29.9: overweight; 30.0–34.9: class I; 35.0–39.9: class II; 40+: class III obesity.

Benefits of monitoring

BMI can help notice broad weight trends and start a useful conversation alongside blood pressure, labs, habits, and medical history.

Important limitations

BMI does not directly measure body fat or its distribution. Muscle, bone, age, sex, and ethnicity can affect what the number means.

Different assessments

Athletes, pregnant people, children, and older adults often need different growth, body-composition, or clinical assessments.

When to seek advice

Speak with a healthcare professional about unexpected weight change, symptoms, pregnancy, eating concerns, or before major diet or exercise changes.
